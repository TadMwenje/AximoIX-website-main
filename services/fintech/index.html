The Problem

Financial technology carries stakes that other software doesn't: regulatory exposure, fraud risk, and the complete erosion of user trust on a single incident.

Most development teams build fintech features as an afterthought — bolting payment processing onto existing platforms, ignoring PCI-DSS scope, and using third-party services without understanding data residency requirements.

AximoIX treats compliance as architecture, not a checklist applied at the end.

Frequently Asked Questions

What compliance standards does AximoIX design for?

AximoIX designs for PCI-DSS (payment card industry), GDPR (data privacy), AML/KYC (anti-money laundering and know-your-customer), and Open Banking standards. Compliance requirements are scoped in the first discovery sprint and architected into the system — not retrofitted at audit time.

Does AximoIX build blockchain and crypto applications?

Yes. AximoIX builds smart contracts on Ethereum and Solana, tokenisation platforms, NFT infrastructure, and DeFi application backends. Regulatory context for the target market is assessed during discovery — AximoIX does not build systems intended to circumvent financial regulation.

Which payment processors does AximoIX integrate with?

AximoIX has integration experience with Stripe, PayPal, Adyen, Square, Braintree, PayFast, and regional processors across Africa, Europe, and North America. Multi-processor routing (for redundancy and regional optimisation) is a standard recommendation on high-volume implementations.

What security measures are standard in AximoIX FinTech builds?

Standard security measures include: tokenisation (no raw card data stored), end-to-end encryption for data in transit and at rest, multi-factor authentication, rate limiting and anomaly detection, role-based access control, comprehensive audit logging, and penetration testing before production launch.
